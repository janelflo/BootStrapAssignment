Phytonutrients: How eating the rainbow can lead to a healthier life

What is a phytonutrient?

According to the Produce for Better Health Foundation, "The term phytonutrients is a broad name for a wide variety of compounds produced by plants. Theyâ€™re found in fruits, vegetables, beans, grains, and other plants. Each phytonutrient comes from a variety of different plant sources and has different proposed effects on, and benefits for, the body. Some researchers estimate there are up to 4,000 phytonutrients! Scientists have identified thousands of them, although only a small fraction of phytonutrients have been studied closely."

In order to experience the healing and nutritional effects of these properties, humans need to consume a variety of fruits and vegetables daily! This can easily be done by grouping produce into colors of the rainbow as shown below:

Red

Sources: strawberries, cranberries, raspberries, tomatoes, cherries, apples, beets, watermelon, red grapes, red peppers, red onions

Orange & Yellow

Sources: carrots, sweet potatoes, yellow peppers, oranges, bananas, pineapple, tangerines, mango, pumpkin, apricots, winter squash, peaches, cantaloupe, corn

Green

Sources: spinach, avocados, asparagus, artichokes, broccoli, alfalfa sprouts, kale, cabbage, Brussels sprouts, kiwi fruit, collard greens, green tea, green herbs

Blue & Purple

Sources: blueberries, blackberries, elderberries, Concord grapes, raisins, eggplant, plums, figs, prunes, lavender, purple cabbage

White & Brown

Sources: onions, cauliflower, garlic, leeks, parsnips, daikon radish, mushrooms
